What the assessment covers

Operational resilience starts with clarity. The assessment identifies what must keep running, what can fail temporarily, and what dependencies create unacceptable risk during cyber disruption.

Minimum viable operation

Define the business, mission, and operational activities that must continue under pressure.

Dependency mapping

Identify the systems, suppliers, decision paths, and recovery assumptions your critical operations depend on.

Priority gap analysis

Pinpoint the governance, technical, and process gaps that most directly threaten continuity.

Best fit for

  • Critical infrastructure operators

    Organizations that must maintain service delivery under attack, degradation, or supply chain interruption.

  • Industrial and OT-heavy environments

    Teams that need resilience priorities grounded in operational reality, not generic IT recovery plans.

  • Leadership preparing for regulatory scrutiny

    Boards and executives that need a clearer resilience baseline for NIS2, IEC 62443, ISO 27001, or internal assurance work.

Typical outcomes

Most assessments finish with a sharper definition of critical operation, a clearer dependency picture, and a shorter list of resilience priorities that leadership can actually act on.

We focus on what must continue, what is most likely to fail, and what decisions become critical when technical degradation starts to spread.
